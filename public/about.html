Features

Everything you need, nothing you don’t

Ride keeps things simple: plan, ride, document, share. No clutter, no subscriptions.

Waypoint Planning

Drop pins on the map, search for places, drag to reorder. Build your route visually and intuitively.

Trip Journal

Log notes, discoveries and memories at every stop. Attach photos and keep a private or public record of your journey.

Share & Collaborate

Generate a short link and share your trip publicly. Friends see your route, waypoints and journal on a beautiful read-only page.

Turn-by-Turn Routing

Automatic road-snapped routes between your waypoints powered by self-hosted OSRM. Real roads, real distances.

Private by Default

Your trips are yours. Nothing is public unless you choose to share. No accounts scraped, no data sold.

Export & Backup

Download your trip as JSON anytime. Your data is portable — take it wherever you want.

How it works

From idea to open road in minutes

No tutorials needed. Just open the app and start.

1

Sign in

Use your Google or Microsoft account — no new password to remember. You’re in immediately.

2

Create a trip

Tap New Trip, give it a name, and you’re looking at a map. Add a cover image to make it yours.

3

Drop waypoints

Click the map or search for a place. Drag waypoints to reorder. The route updates instantly with real road geometry.

4

Journal the ride

At camp, at a lookout, after a great piece of road — add a journal entry with notes and photos. Build a story as you go.

5

Share or keep it private

Toggle your trip to public and get a short link. Or keep it completely private — your call.

6

Hit the road

Enable Ride Mode for real-time GPS tracking along your planned route. The map follows you.

The story

Built by a rider, for riders

Collaboration, saveable and shareable maps and journaling — without any unnecessary bits. Ease of use, excellence of travel, quietness from the usual mainstream apps.

Ride was crafted by a motorbike rider, camper and general interested explorer. The goal was simple: build one clean tool for planning trips, documenting the journey, and sharing routes with others — without the noise, the upsells, or the data harvesting that comes with mainstream apps.

It started as a weekend project and grew into something I reach for on every trip, from a Saturday morning loop through the hills to a multi-day ride across state lines. No sign-up walls, no premium tiers, no ads. Just ride.

You can read more about how this was built, the infrastructure choices, and the broader projects at my blog. If Ride is useful to you and you’d like to support the hosting costs, you can buy me a coffee.

Under the hood

Solid foundations

Ride is built on open-source cartography and global-edge infrastructure.

Leaflet
OpenStreetMap
OSRM
Cloudflare
D1 + R2

Maps rendered by Leaflet with tiles from OpenStreetMap. Routing powered by a self-hosted OSRM instance. Data lives on Cloudflare D1 and R2 at the edge.
